How many calories are in your favorite Mexican dish? The quick guide

From tacos al pastor to mole poblano, here's how the calories in your go-to order really compare — so you stop guessing.

By CalorIA Scan · July 22, 2026 · 2 min read

The short version

  • A mole poblano (480 kcal) can carry more than double the calories of tacos al pastor (220 kcal) —same Mexican craving, very different calorie counts.
  • Portion size changes everything: a chilaquiles plate (480 kcal) can easily match a plate of enchiladas (420 kcal) without you noticing.
  • These numbers are general averages from our own Mexican food database. The only way to know the exact calories in your specific plate is to scan it.

Tacos al pastor — 220 kcal

Mexico's most-ordered craving, and one of the lightest on this list. Three well-served tacos al pastor land around 220 kcal, mostly split between the tortilla, marinated pork, and a bit of pineapple. The real calorie hit almost always comes from what you pile on top: extra cream, extra cheese, or a very oily salsa.

Guacamole — 160 kcal

A standard serving of guacamole runs about 160 kcal —almost all of it good fat from the avocado. It's not the villain at the table; the real issue is usually how many fried chips you're using as the delivery vehicle.

Elote (Mexican street corn) — 180 kcal

A fully dressed elote (mayo, cheese, chili, lime) runs about 180 kcal. The corn itself is barely a third of that —the rest is the mayo and cheese, which you can ask for "light" to bring the number down.

Pozole rojo — 350 kcal

A bowl of red pozole with its meat and hominy corn runs around 350 kcal before you add anything. Lettuce, radish and oregano on top barely move that number —but an extra handful of chicharrón does, fast.

Enchiladas rojas — 420 kcal

Three red enchiladas, sauced and cheesed, add up to roughly 420 kcal. Here, the oil from frying the tortilla before rolling it carries almost as much weight as the filling itself.

Chilaquiles rojos — 480 kcal

A plate of chilaquiles with cream, cheese and an egg on top easily reaches 480 kcal —the same as a full mole poblano. It's the most underestimated breakfast calorie-wise, because "it's just chips".

Mole poblano — 480 kcal

A piece of chicken bathed in mole poblano also runs around 480 kcal, tying with chilaquiles. That tracks: mole is built from chocolate, lard and several kinds of ground chile —it's a dense sauce, not a light one.

Why these numbers are only the starting point

These figures are averages from our own Mexican and Latino food database —useful for comparing cravings against each other, but not for knowing exactly what you ate today. Portion size, whoever's cooking, and whatever you piled on top can swing the final number by 100 kcal or more in either direction.
